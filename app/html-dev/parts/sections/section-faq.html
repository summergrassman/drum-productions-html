1. Do you charge extra for percussion parts?

No. My flat fee is for providing the right part for your song, whether that means a drum set part with no percussion, a hybrid set/percussion part, or percussion only.

The important thing is that whatever I am providing is what the song needs.

2. Do you perovide 1-4 measure loops?

This is a pretty common scenario for me, where a producer might just need a snippet of a beat, perhaps based on an old record sample or something. The fee is the same, as I still need to go through the exact same process of creating the drum set, getting everything miced up, and recording the part. What I generally do in these situations is also send beat and fill variations based on the original loop, as well as individual hits of the drums and cymbals so you have a lot of options for creating your own tracks.

3. Do you create a custom microphone configuration for every song?

I do not leave one drumset setup with the same mics for every song. Each song is unique and each song requires a unique approach in both feel and tonal vibe. I work hard and diligently to deliver the right sound and feel for every song.

5. Do you provide a final mix of the drum tracks?

I will be sending a rough mix along with the individual dry tracks. The rough mix will have basic levels and panning applied, perhaps a bit of compression here and there. If you need a great mix I can do this as well.

6. Do I need to have recorded the song to a click track?

No, it's not absolutely necessary. But I recommend it. Songs not recorded to a click or over 5 minutes will incur an additional charge.
